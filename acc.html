Afrikan Clothing Co.

Freelance Web Design Strategy & Development

Overview

Afrikan Clothing Co. is a social enterprise with the goal of employing women in Kenya to design and construct custom apparel out of wax prints — a traditional African fabric — to be sold via an online store to women in the United States and eventually Europe. All profits from the enterprise will be funneled into a foundation managed by the same women Afrikan Clothing Co. employs. Its purpose will be to distribute funds to programs aimed at helping women and girls in East Africa gain access to education and vocational training.

The foundation received an initial grant from Mercy Corps to get operations up and running, but need additional funding to continue their mission. They plan to raise this capital through the crowdfunding platform StartSomeGood. They asked me to build them a website to serve as a platform for educating people about Afrikan Clothing Co.’s mission, why the focus on East Africa, and direct visitors toward their crowdfunding site.

Problem

In order to procure additional funding, Afrikan Clothing Co. needed a professional website for potential donors to learn about the enterprise, gain a deeper understanding of its mission, and see what kinds of products will be offered in its initial product line. The main goal of the site was to be informative, educating visitors about East Africa and why Kenya in particular is the perfect environment, both socially and economically, to spur positive change in the region.

Solution

The development of a website would help Afrikan Clothing Co. in a number of ways. Not only would it serve as a destination for learning about the enterprise and the products it plans to offer in its first product line, but it would help bring credibility to the brand and to its mission to facilitate change for women and girls in a region of the world often overlooked by western interests.

Research

I relied heavily on the founder of Afrikan Clothing Co. to provide me background on who she sees as its target consumers based off of her own research as well as who she envisions the crowdfunding campaign to be aimed at. I followed up my initial discussion with her with a more formal interview, the answers from which served as a foundation for building personas and user stories to inform the organization and layout of the site.

User Stories

As a user on the Landing page I want to see a…

  • Large CTA that leads back to the donation page on Start Some Good

  • Preview or glipse of the mission of the company

  • Link to the about page

  • Photo feed from Instagram

  • Easy, obvious navigation to other parts of the site

As a user on the About page I want to see a…

  • CTA button that leads to the donation page

  • Text area that talks about the company’s mission

  • Break down all the various sections of the mission: women only, supporting communities, connecting women across the globe, understanding of the foundation

  • Walk-through of how dollars spent on Afrikan Clothing Co. products directly benefit the communities

  • Photo of the founder

  • Area for the story of the founder's connection to fashion, Kenya, and her cause

As a user on the Products page I want to see a…

  • CTA button that leads to the donation page

  • Representation of each product in the initial product line

  • Blurb as to why each of these styles were chosen

  • Example of a wax print

As a user on the Kenya page I want to see a…

  • CTA button that leads to the donation page

  • Map of Kenya and where Nairobi is located in the country

  • Text area to include an explanation as to why Kenya has been selected as the home country for Afrikan Clothing Co. and its potential for impact in East Africa

Website Goal

The goal of Afrikan Clothing Co.’s website is to inform users of its mission, provide them information about its initial product line, teach them about Kenya and East Africa, and convert them to donors to the project.

Wireframes

UX

I wanted the UX of the site to feel straightfoward and contain easily recognizable heuristics for anyone who has spent much time browsing any website - navigation located in the upper right corner, all links identifiable by an underline, and all buttons large and obvious. With the main goal of the site to educate users about the brand and its mission with the hope that they will want to pledge to the cause and help fund the project, I tried to ensure no blocks of text were too large to digest and they were surrounded by beautiful large photos showcasing Kenya, the country this social enterprise hopes to empower. I made sure the crowdfunding CTA boxes were obvious and eye-catching to point users the direction to get involved in the project.

UI

The user interface for the site needed to be clean and elegant so as not to detract from the goal of converting visitors into donors. Accompanying the dark and light greys were a bright yellow used on CTAs and a rich blue for accents, both colors pulled from two of the client’s favorite wax prints.

Digitizing Wax Prints

The client did not have actual examples of her products to photograph and display on the products page since she was still in the midst of obtaining funding to get her company off the ground. She did, however, have flat line drawings of the four products that were to make up her initial product line, as well as swatches of the wax prints she intended to use for these first styles. I thought a creative way to showcase the prints in conjunction with the pieces they were going to be featured on would be to recreate each wax print digitally and show them alongside their corresponding flat line drawing. I did this by breaking each print down into the smallest geometric shapes I could and then layered them together to recreate each print. On the products page I incorporated a parallax effect on each wax print - removing the effect on mobile screen sizes in favor of better usability.
