Our client, a biotechnology company, was coming to market with a revolutionary new cancer treatment. They tasked us with creating an iPad app that could be used by company reps when speaking with doctors to educate them about how the process works, the benefits of it, as well as display its safety and outcome data in an easy to digest way.

All content has been blinded to maintain client confidentiality.

Problem

Our client wanted a digital tool that would allow their reps to quickly and easily walk through all features, safety, and outcome data of their new cancer treatment with doctors. The tool needed to present complex data and information in a way that was simple to navigate, while balancing the large amount of content that was legally required to be present on each screen.

Solution

The finished app allows for reps to easily explain all aspects of our client's new treatment to doctors. It's designed to best facilitate a rep's ability to have a short, detail-rich conversation with doctors and visually show the features and benefits of our client's new cancer treatment.

Scope and constraints

This project needed to be designed and developed in just under 8 months to ensure it went live alongside the launch of our client's cancer treatment. Built into this timing was four rounds of three-week long legal reviews to ensure all claims, data, charts, and functionality passed the FDA’s stringent requirements for medical accuracy.

Design

The design process began with outlining the app's requirements as supplied by our client and their legal team. We translated those into wireframes to establish functionality, followed by the creation of design system components, and finished with high-fidelity mock ups. Between each of these phases we met with our client's legal team to confirm adherence to FDA rules and regulations.

Strategy

For ease of navigation and a customizable flow, each screen of the app would feature condensed, streamlined content with additional supporting data or graphics accessible via screen overlays. This would allow reps to keep their conversations high-level, while having the ability to dig deeper on each topic if the doctor they were speaking with wanted to.

Functionality and wireframes

I created wireframes for every screen within each section to illustrate our proposed structure and functionality to the client. We sent the wireframes through their legal review to ensure our proposed design was approved by our client and their lawyers before moving forward into higher-fidelity layouts.

Functionality

Some of the functional requirements were dictated by our client to keep the app consistent with other tools their reps were familiar with, while others were developed by our team to streamline the app's interface.

  1. Forward and backward buttons allowing reps to jump between top-level content sections
  2. Button to access a menu of top-level content sections
  3. Tapping brand logo brings user home
  4. On-screen tabs to separate content
  5. Zoom button to open a screen overlay to dive deeper into data, graphs, or charts
  6. Button to open screen overlay for additional content
  7. Button to display visual map of all content areas, including overlays
  8. Button to access screen overlay that summarizes key takeaways

Necessary on-screen elements

There were a few elements each screen was required to have from a legal perspective. To keep them unobtrusive, yet accessible if necessary, I kept them all together at the bottom of each screen, reserving the majority of the screen real estate for regularly interacted with elements.

  1. Any applicable footnotes
  2. Important safety information related to that screen's particular content
  3. Ability to access the drug's prescribing information at all times
  4. Any applicable journal references accessible at all times

Design System

All projects in our client’s digital ecosystem needed to have a cohesive look and feel to ensure the brand felt the same across all user touchpoints. To do so, we established a design system that was flexible enough to adapt to multiple digital products. Though this app was the first project our agency started working on, I created a system that was adaptable for all.

Color

We were provided a brand color palette from our client, though had the freedom to bring the colors into the project however we saw fit. Working in collaboration with David Bloom, the lead creative director on the project, I established how color would be applied throughout all of the brand’s digital pieces including typography, charts, graphs, etc.

Graphics and iconography

We landed on a simple line graphic style that used the blue gradient our client loved and brought in a crimson color pop to highlight key areas.

This is a selection of the graphics developed for the project by my agency art partners.

Data callout boxes

I created a reusable design pattern of boxes with white backgrounds and crimson keylines to use when highlighting important data and information throughout the piece. For screens with large amounts of information, I integrated a tab system to break information into more digestible pieces.

Graphs

Our client loved the sapphire blue/deep sky blue gradient. Gradients are great for visually signifying progression, so where applicable I decided to pull it through in graphs to signify change over time.

Tables

There was a need for two types of tables, one to show data and the other to compare two sets of information.

Pie Charts

Two types of pie charts were needed - one that combined multiple pieces of data and another for individual data points.

High-fidelity layouts
